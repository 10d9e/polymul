## Approach

Replace Shoup with Plantard multiplication throughout the NTT
Every constant modular multiply in the forward and inverse transforms
(twiddles, the 4th-root rotations, the psi pre/post weights) switches from
Shoup's method to Plantard's: a single precomputed u64 constant
bprime = (c * (-2^64 mod p) mod p) * p^{-1} mod 2^64, and
  plantard(x) = ((sext_hi32(x*bprime) + (2^32+1)) * p) >> 32   in [0,2p)
This is exact for any non-negative x < 2^32 (verified against x*c mod p
across the full u32 input range and all lazy ranges), so it is a drop-in
for the lazy Shoup. It costs only TWO multiplies instead of three, and
needs ONE constant per twiddle instead of Shoup's (value, shoup) pair,
which also halves the twiddle tables and their per-butterfly loads. The
+q that shifts Plantard's centred result into [0,2p) is folded for free
into the rounding constant (2^32+1). The pointwise product stays
Montgomery (variable x variable) and the Garner CRT stays Shoup.
