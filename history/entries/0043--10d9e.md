## Approach

Vectorize the second inverse DIT sub-stage (dit_l4) inside the fused boundary tile. On a 16-element tile this stage's four radix-4 butterflies (g=0..3) sit at adjacent slots for every leg (g, g+4, g+8, g+12), so a pair (g, g+1) rides the two i64x2 lanes with contiguous v128 loads and no cross-lane shuffles; the two lanes' twiddles (e=64g and 64(g+1)) are loaded as a pair. Reuses the vectorized DIT butterfly already used by the main inverse pass. The other two boundary sub-operations stay scalar: the pointwise product is cross-lane (a*b within one packed vector) and the first DIT sub-stage has butterflies four slots apart. Same two-lane L abstraction; native correctness gate runs the identical algorithm and the wasm checksums match the scalar baseline on all 32 fixtures.
