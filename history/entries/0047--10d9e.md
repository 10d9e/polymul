## Approach

Smaller 27-bit primes unlock lazy reduction removal in the forward NTT
Replace the three ~2^30 NTT primes with three ~2^27 primes (134250497,
134275073, 134330369). Their product is 2^81, still far above the 2^75
needed to recover the exact signed product (|c_k| < 2^74), but the extra
headroom under 2^32 (32x instead of ~4x) means several conditional
reductions that were overflow-mandatory at 30 bits are now removable:
- Forward radix-4 butterfly: s1 = a+2p-c feeds only Shoup multiplies
  (which tolerate any value < 2^32), so it stays lazy in [0,4p); at 30-bit
  primes y1 = s1+is3 < 6p overflowed 2^32, at 27-bit it is < 2^31.
- Boundary last forward stage (dif_l1_v): its outputs feed only the
  pointwise Montgomery product, which tolerates inputs up to ~5.6p
  (K^2 p < 2^32), so all four output reductions are dropped (outputs [0,4p)).
