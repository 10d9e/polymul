## Approach

In the forward radix-4 butterfly, the sum terms u=x0+x2 and v=x1+x3 were each conditionally reduced to [0,p), then combined. But u and v feed only the output i0=(u+v) and i1=(u-v)*tc, both of which end in a % p. Keep u,v lazily in [0,2p) and let the output reductions handle it: i0=(u+v)%p and i1=((u+2p-v)*tc)%p (the +2p keeps the difference non-negative; products stay < 2^63). This removes two conditional subtractions per butterfly across all forward passes (r4_dif, the pre-weighted first pass, and the q=1 pass). ~10% fewer fuel operators; outputs unchanged.
