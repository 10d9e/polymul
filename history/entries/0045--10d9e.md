## Approach

Eliminate the per-butterfly splat in the forward NTT. The forward pairs operands a and b in the two i64x2 lanes and multiplies both by the SAME twiddle, so each butterfly broadcast a scalar twiddle to a vector with a separate i64x2.splat. By storing the forward-only tables (psi, psis, w, ws) as u64 instead of u32, the broadcast 'L::splat(*ptr)' now lowers to a single v128.load64_splat instruction — load and splat fused, no extra op. Also hoists the constant forward 4th-root broadcast out of the butterfly. The inverse tables stay u32 (its lanes pair adjacent butterflies with different twiddles, loaded as pairs). Same two-lane L abstraction; native correctness gate runs the identical algorithm and the wasm checksums match the scalar baseline on all 32 fixtures.
