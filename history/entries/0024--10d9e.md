## Approach

Rewrite the 3-prime negacyclic NTT to be **division-free** under the weighted
cost model (where an i64 `%`/divide costs ~25x an add, as on real hardware).
Every modular multiply by a precomputed constant — the radix-4 twiddles, the
psi negacyclic pre/post weights, and the Garner CRT mixing constants — uses
**Shoup's method**: with `c' = floor(c*2^32/p)` precomputed (free, in plan_new),
`x*c mod p` is `q = (x*c')>>32; r = x*c - q*p; if r>=p {r-p} else {r}` — a couple
of multiplies, a shift and a conditional subtract, but **no division**. Sums are
kept eagerly reduced in [0,p) with a conditional subtract (~2 ops) rather than
deferred to a later `%` (the old lazy-reduction trick, which the weighted metric
correctly penalizes). Radix-4 DIF forward / DIT inverse (1024 = 4^5, five
passes), no bit-reversal. Only the spectral pointwise product (both operands
variable) and the per-input coefficient reduction still use `%`.
